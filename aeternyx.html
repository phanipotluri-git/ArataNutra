Prescribing Safety

Clinical Cautions &
Drug Interactions

⚠ Critical — All Specialties
K2 MK-7 × Warfarin / VKAs

K2 MK-7 DIRECTLY interacts with warfarin and all vitamin K antagonists — INR will be affected. Mandatory disclosure to anticoagulation team, INR monitoring, and dose adjustment.

✓ Safe with all NOACs — apixaban, rivaroxaban, dabigatran
⚠ Critical — Endocrinology · Cardiology
ALA × Insulin / All Antidiabetics

ALA potentiates ALL insulin and oral antidiabetics via AMPK/GLUT4. Mandatory blood glucose monitoring at initiation. Highest risk in fasted insulin-dependent patients.

⚠ Critical — Mandatory Screen
Resveratrol × CYP3A4 / CYP2C9

Resveratrol inhibits CYP3A4 (>50%), CYP2C9, and CYP1A2. Affected: immunosuppressants (tacrolimus, cyclosporin), antivirals, statins, warfarin, amiodarone, antiepileptics (phenytoin).

⚠ Critical — Reproductive
Resveratrol × Pregnancy

NOT recommended during pregnancy or breastfeeding. Stop at least 1 month before planned conception. Replace immediately with dedicated prenatal supplement (folate 5mg, iron, iodine, calcium).

⚠ Critical — Transplant Patients
Resveratrol × Calcineurin Inhibitors

AVOID in transplant patients on tacrolimus or cyclosporin. CYP3A4 inhibition may dangerously elevate immunosuppressant levels — risk of rejection or nephrotoxicity/CNS toxicity.

⚠ Critical — All Surgical Patients
Perioperative Protocol

STOP 7–14 days pre-op: Resveratrol, Tocotrienol, CoQ10. STOP 24–48 hrs: ALA (disclose to anaesthesiologist). SAFE: Vitamin C, Selenium, D3, K2. RESUME Day 5–7 post-haemostasis.

⚡ Caution — Nephrology CKD 3b+
Vitamin C × Advanced CKD

40 mg dose is safe for CKD stages 1–3a. In CKD 3b–5 and dialysis: Vitamin C metabolises to oxalate which accumulates in renal failure. Limit total Vitamin C ≤100 mg/day.

⚡ Caution — Granulomatous Disease
D3 × Sarcoidosis / Active TB

Activated macrophages in granulomatous disease produce unregulated 1,25-OH D3 causing hypercalcaemia. Exogenous D3 can worsen this — monitor serum calcium before supplementing.

⚡ Caution — Oncology
Antioxidants × Chemotherapy

Avoid all antioxidants within 24–48 hours of chemotherapy infusion unless specifically cleared by the treating oncologist. Resveratrol CYP3A4 may alter sorafenib and tamoxifen levels.
